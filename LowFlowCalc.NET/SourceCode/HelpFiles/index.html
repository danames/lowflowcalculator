Overview

Low Flow Calculator™ computes a streamflow value that represents an average low flow period for a specific number of days with a given return period based on a historical data set of daily streamflow observations.

Water quality modelers are often interested in the "7Q10" streamflow as a representative dry condition to use in modeling, particularly in cases where water quality is worst during low flow periods (e.g. if it is dominated by point sources such as wastewater treatment plants.) The 7Q10 streamflow is the yearly 7-day average low streamflow with a 10-year return period.

Low Flow Calculator™ can also be used to compute low streamflow periods other than 7Q10. For example, you can use Low Flow Calculator™ to compute the expected low flow that will occur over a two week period once every 100 years (i.e. the 100 year - two-week drought), or the one-day low flow that will occur every 10 years.

Additionally, while the Low Flow Calculator™ is primarily used for computing low streamflow with given return periods, it can also be used to compute the expected high streamflow, or flood, with a given return period.  By choosing "High flow" from the "Options|Change Parameters" menu, one can compute such quantities as the 100-year 4-day flood.
